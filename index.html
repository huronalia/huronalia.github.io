Problem 1: Media Fills Suck

The FDA mandates a media fill inspection, at least once every six months, for at minimum 10,000 vials per filling line. Each vial is filled, cultured, and inspected by hand slowly and carefully for potential contamination. Most of the time a perfectly sterile, clear vial of media is picked up, looked at, and put down. This takes considerable time and costs a considerable amount of money.

More than just text, artificial intelligence models can process visual information at extremely high accuracy and at superhuman speed. They are so fast that comparatively, most of the time spent in a conventional automated visual analysis machine is the physical: unloading a tray, gently handling each vial, and loading each back another tray. Each vial is inspected sequentially, one at a time and therefore, the process is slow.

The FDA is quickly solidifying its stance on AI in regulated industries. It can provide real benefit for regulatory compliance and firms that adopt AI early will have a disproportionate advantage. We have created a device, the PVA 700, which at its core leverages AI to detect and asses vials at speeds no other automated visual inspection machine can match.

OUR SOLUTION: The PVA 700

----> Our innovation: process multiple vials at the same time

We have created an affordable benchtop media fill analysis device. Our device processes vials in parallel, without removal from the tray. This allows us to dramatically reduce cost and speed analysis. Our theoretical parallel vial analysis (PVA) speed is greater than 700 vials per minute. We do this by agitating and imaging vials in the tray. A single camera frame captures each vial in a tray and our algorithms can nearly instantly assess each vial for turbidity.
